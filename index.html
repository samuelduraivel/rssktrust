About Us

Ramakrishna Sharada Shakti Kendra was founded on September 21, 1996, by Shri M. Vasudeva Hande, who served as the Author and Founder of the Trust. Over the years, the Trust has evolved to adapt to changing circumstances and continue its charitable mission.

Our activities benefit all sections of the public without distinction of religion, caste, creed, or other denominations, ultimately serving the whole world.

Vision & Mission

Vision: To stimulate people's involvement in social and cultural development while preserving and propagating the spiritual heritage of India.

Mission:

  • To broadcast the inspiring life and message of Sri Ramakrishna, Holy Mother Sharada Devi, and Swami Vivekananda.
  • To provide opportunities for spiritual growth through meditation, discourses, and study circles.
  • To support youth through conventions and thought-provoking activities.
  • To promote literacy and knowledge through libraries and publications.
  • To foster moral education and ethical values in society.
  • To offer a path for those desiring to renounce social life for spiritual pursuit.

Objectives & Activities

Healthcare & Welfare

  • Operating a dedicated hospital to provide medical care to the community.
  • Running an old age home to offer shelter, care, and dignity to the elderly.
  • Providing accessible ambulance services for medical emergencies.
  • Supporting an orphanage to provide a safe, nurturing environment and education for children.

Spiritual Activities

  • Organizing and conducting spiritual camps for the general public.
  • Holding sessions on meditation, spiritual topics, discussions, and discourses.

Youth Development

  • Organizing youth conventions for students to encourage moral and spiritual values.
  • Conducting thought-provoking competitions to stimulate intellectual and spiritual growth.

Publications & Education

  • Printing and publishing books and booklets containing the teachings of the Holy Trio.
  • Organizing and conducting public libraries to promote knowledge and learning.

Get Involved

  • Donations: Monetary contributions (eligible for 80G tax benefit), donations in kind, endowments, or program sponsorships.
  • Volunteering: Help organize spiritual camps, assist in library management, support youth conventions, or contribute to publication efforts.
  • Participation: Attend spiritual camps, meditation sessions, study circles, and youth conventions.
  • Collaboration: We welcome partnerships with social organizations, educational institutions, and cultural bodies.
